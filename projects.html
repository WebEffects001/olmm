Aviation, Transportation & Ports

OLMM has worked extensively to provide structural engineering services involving the design of airport upgrades, expansion, and public transportation facilities.

Terminal-2, San Francisco International Airport


Structural engineering services to study the feasibility of rehabilitating the former International Terminal (Central Terminal, Control Tower, Concourse, and Boarding Area-D). OLMM performed detailed seismic evaluation and developed conceptual strengthening schemes using essential facilities seismic criteria to ensure continued operations of the Control Tower during a major earthquake. This is LEED Gold Project.

Client: Gensler



OLMM received the Ideas2 National Certificate from AISC, Excellence in Structural Engineering Certificate of Merit from SEAOC and Architectural Engineering Project of the Year from ASCE San Francisco Section.


New International Terminal, San Francisco International Airport


Structural engineering services for the five-story, 1 million sq. ft. steel frame boarding areas flanking each side of the main International Terminal. Design was performed to meet the performance-based seismic criteria requiring the facility to remain operational during a major earthquake.

Client: SOM



OLMM received the Excellence in Structural Engineering Award from SEAOC and SEAONC for this project.


Terminal T3 West Expansion, San Francisco International Airport


Design of foundations, piles, pile caps and ground level structural slabs to receive loading from baggage handling equipment and two-story steel terminal expansion building above. Utilized BIM and FEM technologies to exchange both design and coordination information with various team disciplines and team members. Project is currently under design phase.

Client: Magnusson Klemencic Associates



Terminal-2 Extension, Oakland International Airport


Structural engineering services for the new 2-story, 110,000 sq. ft. steel frame concourse building using performance based seismic design criteria. OLMM’s value engineering ideas in the selection of structural system led to almost $2 million in cost savings and 6 months in construction time. OLMM contributed in significant and innovative ways to help the project achieve LEED certification. This is a LEED Silver project.

Client: Jacobs / Carter Burgess
